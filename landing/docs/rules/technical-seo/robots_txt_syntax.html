robots.txt has valid syntax

medium

robots_txt_syntax

Last updated: 2026-06-24

Checks that robots.txt has valid syntax.

What does this rule check?

Robots.txt follows a specific syntax format. Invalid syntax (missing User-agent, malformed directives, wrong line endings) can cause crawlers to misinterpret your instructions.

Why is this important?

Invalid syntax may cause crawlers to ignore your directives entirely, leading to unwanted crawling of private areas or blocking of important pages.

How to fix

Fix syntax errors in your robots.txt. Ensure each group starts with a User-agent line. Use standard directives: Allow, Disallow, Sitemap.

Examples

Incorrect 
Disallow: /admin/
<!-- Missing User-agent line! -->

user agent: Googlebot
<!-- Wrong syntax! -->
Correct 
User-agent: *
Disallow: /admin/

User-agent: Googlebot
Allow: /

2. Use "Disallow:" and "Allow:" (capitalized, with colon)
